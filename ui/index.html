🌍 Green AI & Sustainability Measures

Every step of our pipeline is designed with environmental responsibility in mind.

🔋 Carbon-Budget Training

Surrogate models train under a strict carbon budget. Training automatically stops if the budget is exceeded, preventing wasteful computation. Estimated emissions: ~0.643 kg CO₂ (US grid) or ~1.252 kg CO₂ (India grid) per full pipeline run.

⏰ Early Stopping

All neural networks use patience-based early stopping (patience=20 epochs). S_bio stopped at epoch 69 (of 300 max), S_mech at epoch 73 — saving ~75% of potential training compute.

💧 Water Footprint Tracking

Data center cooling requires water. We estimate ~2.75L water per pipeline run and track this alongside carbon emissions in our Green AI report.

📊 TDP-Based Estimation

Instead of requiring hardware power meters, we use processor TDP (Thermal Design Power) ratings to estimate energy consumption — making sustainability tracking accessible to all researchers. Total energy: 1.527 kWh.

♻️ Biodegradable Focus

The entire purpose: replacing plastics that persist for 500+ years with alternatives that biodegrade in months. Each successful polymer candidate prevents decades of microplastic pollution. Top candidate degrades 918× faster than PET.

🧬 Active Learning Efficiency

Instead of generating millions of molecules randomly, our GFlowNet focuses exploration on high-reward chemical space. 4,624 candidates generated with 12 active learning rounds refining the search — 1000× more efficient than brute-force approaches.

📈 Real Data Prioritization

By integrating 752 experimentally validated data points (with 2× weighting) from PolyInfo and PN2S databases, we reduce the need for large synthetic datasets and improve model accuracy with less data — fewer epochs, less compute, better results.

⚙️ Discovery Pipeline

Hover over each phase to learn what happens at every step

📊Data PrepPhase 1

Phase 1 — Data Preparation

Converts 8,922+ molecules (SMILES strings) into molecular graphs with 38-dim atom features and 7-dim bond features. Includes 752 real experimentally validated polymers from PolyInfo and PN2S databases, plus 126 curated expert-labeled molecules.
🧬Surrogate
TrainingPhase 2

Phase 2 — Surrogate Models

Trains two neural "judge" models: S_bio (biodegradability predictor, MPNN) and S_mech (mechanical properties, Attentive MPNN). Real data gets 2× weight. Early stopping saves ~67% compute.
🤖GFlowNet
TrainingPhase 3

Phase 3 — GFlowNet Training

Trains the Generative Flow Network (1.6M params) with Trajectory Balance loss. Learns to generate molecules proportional to a multi-objective reward = S_bio^0.35 × S_mech^0.50 × S_syn^0.15.
🔬GenerationPhase 4

Phase 4 — Molecule Generation

Generates 4,624 candidate molecules using the trained GFlowNet. Evaluates validity (100%), diversity (0.805), novelty (99.96%), and reward distribution. Blocks chemically impossible bonds (N-O, N-N, O-O).
🔄Active
LearningPhase 5

Phase 5 — Active Learning

12 rounds of generate → simulate (UFF molecular dynamics) → retrain cycle. Each round refines the surrogates with newly discovered molecules, progressively improving prediction accuracy. Total: 12,000 generated, 960 MD-validated, 84.2% cumulative improvement.
🌿DiscoveryPhase 6

Phase 6 — Polymer Discovery

Given a target plastic (e.g., PET), finds the best biodegradable alternatives. Filters by polymerizability, validates via MD simulation, ranks by mechanical match and biodegradation improvement.
🌍Green AIPhase 7 →

Phase 7 — Green AI Report

Tracks CO₂ emissions (~0.3 kg/run), energy usage, and water footprint. Generates a sustainability scorecard. Click to learn more!

🧪 How It Works

This system uses Generative Flow Networks (GFlowNets) to search ~1060 possible molecules for biodegradable polymer alternatives. The AI simultaneously optimizes biodegradability, mechanical strength, and synthesizability — trained on 752 real-world experiments + 8,922 total molecules. Every candidate is checked for polymerizability — can it actually be made into a real polymer? Top candidate (BP-001) degrades 918× faster than PET while maintaining 30.4 MPa tensile strength.
